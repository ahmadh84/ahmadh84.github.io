Computer Vision is a field where complexity of the problem really matters.

I think OpenCV at the moment lacks some key unsupervised image segmentation methods (superpixels). I usually avoid OpenCV when I know I would need superpixels for the system I am building. Here is what I propose to implement in OpenCV:

  1. Normalized-Cuts [1] - this is one of the most well-studied over-segmentation algorithms out of there. It is basically a spectral clustering algorithm - so it would be useful for other clustering applications.
  2. Felzenszwalb and Huttenlocher [2] - arguably the most used superpixel method out there. I think the reason for its popularity lies in its simplicity. Moreover, unlike Normalized cuts, its space/time complexity allows working on large images.
  3. SLIC Superpixels [3] - A new method which creates superpixels by linear iterative clustering (uses k-means with a limited search space). This would stand as a state-of-the-art method for generating superpixels.
The nice thing about these methods is the ability to play around with the affinity measures between two pixels. We can supply a color, intensity, distance, and even an RGBD based affinity function.

One might ask why implement (2) when (3) already beats the others in performance (see Table 1 [3])? I think (2) is a good step toward having a streaming video segmentation algorithm inside OpenCV. Both Grundmann et al. and Xu et al. [4,5] use (2) as a basis for doing hierarchical video segmentation. When implementing (2), I will make sure it is later extensible to videos.

[1] J. Shi, J. Malik, "Normalized Cuts and Image Segmentation," PAMI 2000
[2] P. Felzenszwalb, D. Huttenlocher, "Efficient Graph-Based Image Segmentation," IJCV 2004
[3] R. Achanta, A. Shaji, K. Smith, A. Lucchi, P. Fua, "SLIC Superpixel Compared to State-of-Art Superpixels Methods," PAMI 2012
[4] M. Grundmann, V. Kwatra, M. Han, I. Essa, "Efficient Hierarchical Graph-Based Video Segmentation," CVPR 2010
[5] C. Xu, C. Xiong, J. Corso, "Streaming Hierarchical Video Segmentation," ECCV 2012
